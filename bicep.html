Bicep Workouts

Long Head Workouts

Bicep Curls

Bicep curls are a fundamental exercise for isolating and developing the biceps. They can be performed with various equipment, including dumbbells, barbells, or cables.

Movement Category: Primary

Programming: 3-4 sets of 10-15 repetitions

Weight: Use a weight that allows you to complete the set with good form.

To do bicep curls:

  • Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand with your palms facing forward.
  • Keeping your elbows close to your torso, curl the weights up towards your shoulders.
  • Slowly lower the weights back to the starting position.
  • Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Incline Curls

Incline curls are performed on an incline bench to target the lower part of the biceps and increase overall bicep strength.

Movement Category: Primary

Programming: 3-4 sets of 10-15 repetitions

Weight: Use a weight that allows for a full range of motion.

To do incline curls:

  • Set an incline bench at about 45 degrees.
  • Sit back on the bench with your arms hanging down, holding dumbbells.
  • Curl the weights up while keeping your upper arms stationary.
  • Slowly lower the weights back to the starting position.

Short Head Workouts

Concentration Curls

Concentration curls are great for focusing on the peak of the biceps and improving muscle definition.

Movement Category: Primary

Programming: 3-4 sets of 10-15 repetitions

Weight: Use a weight that allows you to perform the exercise with strict form.

To do concentration curls:

  • Sit on a bench with your legs apart, and rest your elbow on your inner thigh.
  • Hold a dumbbell with your palm facing up and curl it towards your shoulder.
  • Slowly lower the dumbbell back to the starting position.
  • Repeat for the desired number of repetitions, then switch arms.

Spider Curls

Spider curls are excellent for isolating the biceps and minimizing shoulder involvement during the curl.

Movement Category: Primary

Programming: 3-4 sets of 8-12 repetitions

Weight: Use a weight that challenges your biceps without compromising form.

To do spider curls:

  • Lay face down on an incline bench set at a 45-degree angle.
  • Hold dumbbells with your arms hanging down.
  • Curl the weights up towards your shoulders.
  • Slowly lower the weights back to the starting position.

Overall Biceps Workouts

Reverse Curls

Reverse curls focus on the brachioradialis and can help improve overall arm strength and appearance.

Movement Category: Secondary

Programming: 3-4 sets of 10-15 repetitions

Weight: Use a weight that allows for controlled movements.

To do reverse curls:

  • Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a barbell with an overhand grip.
  • Curl the barbell up towards your shoulders while keeping your elbows stationary.
  • Slowly lower the barbell back to the starting position.

Zotman Curls

Zotman curls combine a regular curl with a reverse curl, targeting both the biceps and forearms.

Movement Category: Primary

Programming: 3-4 sets of 10-15 repetitions

Weight: Use a weight that challenges both the biceps and forearms.

To do zotman curls:

  • Start by curling the dumbbells up with a standard grip.
  • At the top of the curl, rotate your wrists to an overhand grip and lower the weights.

Cable Curls

Cable curls provide constant tension throughout the range of motion, which is great for muscle growth.

Movement Category: Primary

Programming: 3-4 sets of 10-15 repetitions

Weight: Adjust the weight according to your strength level.

To do cable curls:

  • Stand in front of a cable machine with a bar attachment.
  • Grab the bar with an underhand grip and curl it towards your shoulders.
  • Slowly lower the bar back to the starting position.

Alternating Curls

Alternating curls allow you to focus on each arm individually and can help correct strength imbalances.

Movement Category: Primary

Programming: 3-4 sets of 10-15 repetitions

Weight: Use a weight that allows you to perform each curl with proper form.

To do alternating curls:

  • Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand.
  • Alternate curling one dumbbell at a time while keeping the other arm extended.
  • Lower the dumbbell back to the starting position after each repetition.
